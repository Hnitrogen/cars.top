Next‑gen GT Flagship: Acceleration, Braking, Lateral Limit

By Editorial Team 2025‑10‑21 12,480 reads 32 comments

This GT flagship pushes lightweighting and aerodynamics to the extreme. We ran 0–100 sprints, 100–0 braking, moose test, and a 15‑lap stint on a closed track. Here are the key numbers and takeaways.

Acceleration & Launch Control

Launch control offers four preset RPMs. The best 3.1 s came at setting 3 with tire temps in the 70–80°C window. AWD torque split is more aggressive, yielding a calmer squat.

Takeaway: Launch strategy is meaningfully improved, with tighter variance than the last gen. For street, skip launch; use Sport+ starts to preserve driveline.

Braking & Heat Fade

Bigger rotors and higher‑mu pads deliver a 100–0 stop in 31.9 m. After 10 successive stops, pedal travel grew ~8%—fade remains controlled.

Pros

  • Linear initial bite, easy to modulate
  • Good fade resistance
  • More even brake temperature distribution

Cons

  • Higher dust output → more wheel cleaning
  • Cold‑start squeal slightly noticeable

Aero & Lateral Limit

Splitter, diffuser, and active wing add downforce. Moose test hits 81 km/h; lateral peaks at 1.12 g with crisper body response versus last gen.

Buying Advice

If you value stable track‑day performance with street usability, this update is worthwhile. Options: prioritize Track Pack and ceramic brakes; choose lightweight wheels based on budget.
